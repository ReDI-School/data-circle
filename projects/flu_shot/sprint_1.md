# Sprint 1: Exploratory Data Analysis
## Week 1–3 

### Overview
In this sprint, teams will focus on understanding the dataset, cleaning the data, and preparing it for modeling. The goal is to gain insights into vaccination patterns and identify potentially important features.

1. Set up project environment, GitHub repository, and documentation  

2. Define your hypotheses. For example:  
- Beliefs influence vaccination decisions  
- Demographics are key drivers  
- Preventive behaviours correlate with uptake  
- Risk perception affects decisions  
- Vaccination behavior is correlated across vaccines

3. Define research questions. For example:  
- Are certain age groups more likely to get vaccinated?
- Do gender, education, or income affect vaccination?
- Are people with chronic illnesses more likely to get vaccinated?
- Are people who get the seasonal flu vaccine also likely to get H1N1?
- Do household income or employment status correlate with vaccination?

4. Clean and prepare the data. It may include the following steps:
- Validate consistency and data quality
- Remove duplicate entries and entries with missing values for the dependent variable
- Explore outliers
- Reorganise categorical variables with many categories
- Any other steps you find necessary to complete

### Deliverables
- Hypothesis and research questions  
- Key insights and visualizations  
- Reproducible scripts and documentation
- Pitch-presentation (5-minute presentation of results achieved in this sprint)
